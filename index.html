Research

Papers


  • Minimal Ultrasound Imaging System to Reconstruct Uterus Geometry
    collaborated with Global Health Labs and the Gates Foundation.
    In preparation; [Updating...]
    Using seven markers on patients' abdomen we collect anterior/posterior ultrasound imaging data through markers. We study whether using ultrasound data can accurately reconstruct uterine surface, compared to the MRI images. We also quantify shape difference via uterine surface potential maps, electrograms, and the percentage of myometrium that is electrically activated.

  • Global COVID-19 Pandemic Waves: Limited Lessons Learned Worldwide over the Past Year
    with Dr. Y. Wei, J. Guan, X. Ning, L. Wei.
    Accepted at Engineering.; see published version.
    We used Time-varying hierarchical clustering models, regression analysis and data visualization to analyze eight types of performance of 170 countries in the world.

  • Split-step balanced θ-method for stochastic differential equations under non-global Lipschitz conditions
    with Dr. W. Cao, Y. Liu.
    Accepted at Applied Mathematics and Computation.; see published version.
    We study the convergence and stability of an implicit numerical method for stochastic differential equations under non-global Lipschitz conditions. The split-step balanced θ method (SSBT) for solving SDEs is constructed. The boundedness of the numerical solution of the SSBT scheme has been presented. Furthermore, the strong convergence, contraction and stability in mean square sense of the SSBT scheme are proved. Numerical examples are simulated to illustrate the theoretical results.

  • Potential Relationship between Green News and the Stock Movements: An Analysis with Natural Language Processing and Machine Learning
    advised by Dr. Greg Eirich and Dr. Elena Krumova. Columbia M.A. thesis, Fall 2020.
    The thesis aims to investigate any predictive relationship between “green” news (i.e., news related to clean energy, climate change, etc.) and the stock movements of related companies/industries. The thesis applied several NLP and machine learning techniques to news datasets and stock/ETF price data from Yahoo Finance.

Other Projects

  • Development of Ultrasound-based Electromyometrial Imaging System
    Ph.D. project 1. We developed the ultrasound-based electromyometrial imaging system, which is more feasible in clinical setting. We validated that the ultrasound method can generate reasonable uterus shape regarding patient's posture, and accurate uterine potential map.

  • Bayesian Optimization Algorithm, Hyperparameters Tuning, and Performance Comparison
    Course project. We investigated the behaviors of Bayesian optimization, a practical and successful method applied in SVM and deep neural networks in the context of a classification problem with a focus on Bayesian Optimization for hyperparameter tuning.
